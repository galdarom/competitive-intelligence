What Accelerated

Cybersecurity Segment Growth Outperforms

Cybersecurity revenue grew 18.3% YoY in Q1 FY2026, dramatically outpacing Service Assurance (+1.4%). This signals that NETSCOUT’s Arbor product line is the primary growth driver. Consecutive earnings beats (Q1, Q2, Q3) and raised guidance reflect improving execution in the security business.

AI/ML Integration Into Arbor Portfolio

Feb 2026 release added AI-powered Adaptive DDoS Protection for AED, ML-driven ATLAS Intelligence Feed enhancements, and automated threat actor tracking (65+ groups). NETSCOUT is embedding AI into detection and mitigation workflows, not just marketing. The Sightline 9.12 release with 1-second carpet bombing detection is a meaningful technical advance.

Threat Intelligence Leadership Deepens

The H2 2025 DDoS Threat Intelligence Report is the most detailed in the industry: 8M+ attacks catalogued, 30 Tbps peak, 42% multi-vector, 219% AI tool growth. ATLAS visibility into 800+ Tbps of traffic across 125+ countries (500+ ISPs and 3,000+ enterprise sites) provides unmatched telemetry — representing approximately half of all internet activity at any given moment. This research-driven demand generation mirrors Akamai’s SOTI playbook.

What Is New

Automated DDoS Campaign Tracking

ATLAS Intelligence Feed now automatically tracks active campaigns from 65+ known DDoS threat actors and pushes blocking rules to Arbor Edge Defense without operator intervention. This is a step-change in automation that reduces mean-time-to-mitigate for known actor campaigns.

Enhanced Carrier Partnerships

Arelion partnership expansion demonstrates that Tier-1 ISPs continue to choose NETSCOUT Arbor for backbone-level DDoS protection. A 16-year relationship deepened with enhanced carpet-bombing detection. This ISP entrenchment is difficult for competitors to replicate.

What Slowed or Faces Risk

Total Revenue Growth Remains Muted

Despite cybersecurity momentum, total revenue was flat in Q3 FY2026 (-0.5% YoY) due to Service Assurance drag. FY2025 was -0.8%. The company remains dependent on a non-growing legacy business for 63% of revenue. NETSCOUT cannot rerate until cybersecurity growth fully offsets service assurance stagnation.

No WAAP / API Security Offering

NETSCOUT has no WAF, WAAP, bot management, or API security product. As DDoS converges with application security (Cloudflare, Akamai, Radware all offer unified WAAP + DDoS), NETSCOUT’s pure DDoS positioning limits its addressable market and deal sizes in enterprise security consolidation cycles.

Cloud-Native DDoS Competition Intensifies

Cloudflare’s 500 Tbps unmetered DDoS (free with platform), Akamai Prolexic at 20+ Tbps dedicated, and Radware’s hybrid model all pressure NETSCOUT’s traditional ISP/enterprise deployment model. Cloud-native buyers increasingly choose platform-integrated DDoS over best-of-breed appliance/cloud scrubbing approaches.

Meaningful Pattern Shifts

From Network Monitoring Company to DDoS Security Company

NETSCOUT’s identity is shifting. Cybersecurity grew 18.3% vs Service Assurance at 1.4%. The investor narrative, product investment, and market recognition (G2, Frost & Sullivan) all center on Arbor DDoS. The company’s future depends on accelerating this transition. If cybersecurity reaches ~50% of revenue in FY2027, NETSCOUT could earn a security-company valuation multiple.

DDoS Attacks Become AI-Augmented

NETSCOUT’s threat report documents a 219% increase in malicious AI tools and DDoS-for-hire services using conversational AI. Attacks are multi-vector (42%), AI-orchestrated, and reaching 30 Tbps scale. This complexity increase favors dedicated DDoS specialists like NETSCOUT over vendors offering DDoS as an ancillary feature.

Strategic Signals

Signal 1: NETSCOUT’s Cybersecurity Growth Is Real — But Trapped Inside a Flat-Revenue Company

Cybersecurity grew 18.3% in Q1 FY2026 while the company overall was flat (-0.8% FY2025). Three consecutive earnings beats and raised guidance suggest the Arbor business is genuinely accelerating. The strategic risk for Radware is underestimating NETSCOUT because of its muted total revenue — the security-specific business is growing at competitive rates. If NETSCOUT successfully spins off or separates the cybersecurity segment, the standalone Arbor business (~$305M annualized, growing 18%+) would be a focused, well-resourced DDoS competitor.

Signal 2: ATLAS Threat Intelligence Is NETSCOUT’s Strategic Moat

Monitoring 400+ Tbps of internet traffic across 214 countries gives NETSCOUT unmatched DDoS telemetry. The H2 2025 threat report (8M+ attacks catalogued, 30 Tbps peak, 65+ threat actors tracked) is the industry’s most comprehensive DDoS dataset. This telemetry advantage feeds directly into product differentiation — Adaptive DDoS Protection and automated campaign blocking rely on data no competitor can replicate. For Radware, this means competing on DDoS intelligence requires either building comparable telemetry or partnering for it.

Signal 3: The “Pure DDoS” Market Is Shrinking as Platforms Bundle Protection

Cloudflare offers unmetered DDoS included with every plan. Akamai bundles Prolexic with CDN and security contracts. Even Radware and Imperva offer DDoS within WAAP platforms. NETSCOUT’s pure-play DDoS positioning faces structural headwinds as enterprise security consolidation favors platform vendors over point solutions. NETSCOUT’s ISP/carrier channel (Arelion, Tier-1 ISPs) is more defensible than enterprise direct sales where platform consolidation hits hardest. Corero faces the same pure-play structural pressure at much smaller scale ($24M FY2024 revenue), and is responding via channel partnerships (HPE-Juniper, Akamai Prolexic OEM, GTT) rather than platform expansion.

Signal 4: AI-Augmented DDoS Attacks Favor Dedicated Defenders

NETSCOUT’s threat report documents a 219% increase in malicious AI tool mentions and DDoS-for-hire with conversational AI. Attacks reach 30 Tbps with 42% using multi-vector approaches. Rising attack complexity could reverse the platform-bundling trend — when DDoS becomes sophisticated enough, organizations may need a DDoS specialist alongside their platform vendor. This is NETSCOUT’s best strategic argument and Radware’s too: as attacks get harder, generalist DDoS mitigation isn’t enough.

Signal 5: NETSCOUT’s ISP/Carrier Channel Is Its Most Defensible Asset

The Arelion partnership (16 years, expanded 2024–2026) illustrates NETSCOUT’s deep ISP entrenchment. Arbor Sightline + TMS is the de facto standard for Tier-1 ISP backbone DDoS management. No competitor — including Radware — has comparable ISP-level deployment depth. This channel is insulated from enterprise platform-bundling trends because ISPs buy DDoS detection at the network layer, not the application layer. Radware’s hybrid on-prem + cloud model competes in enterprise, not ISP backbone.

Signal 6: NETSCOUT’s Lack of WAAP Limits Deal Size but Simplifies Competitive Positioning

NETSCOUT doesn’t compete with Radware on WAF, bot management, or API security. It competes exclusively on DDoS. In enterprise deals where DDoS is purchased separately from WAAP, NETSCOUT is a direct Radware competitor. In consolidated WAAP + DDoS deals, NETSCOUT is not a factor. The competitive implication: Radware’s unified DDoS + WAAP + API security positioning is stronger than NETSCOUT’s in consolidation cycles, but NETSCOUT’s DDoS depth is stronger in DDoS-specific evaluations. Corero shares NETSCOUT’s pure-play DDoS constraint (no WAAP/API/bot) but at a fraction of the scale — in DDoS-only evaluations Corero is a credible third pure-play option alongside NETSCOUT, especially in service-provider/MSP segments.

Executive Takeaways

1

NETSCOUT Arbor is an underestimated DDoS competitor with accelerating cybersecurity growth

While total revenue is flat (~$823M), cybersecurity grew 18.3% and the company beat estimates three consecutive quarters. The Arbor DDoS business is growing at rates comparable to or exceeding Radware’s. Radware should not dismiss NETSCOUT based on muted headline revenue — the security-specific trajectory warrants close monitoring.

2

NETSCOUT’s ATLAS telemetry is the deepest DDoS intelligence asset in the market

800+ Tbps visibility, 125+ countries, 500+ ISPs, 3,000+ enterprise sites, 65+ threat actor tracking, 8M+ attacks catalogued. No competitor has comparable DDoS-specific telemetry. This feeds Adaptive DDoS Protection and automated campaign blocking. Radware’s competing intelligence advantage is its Global Threat Report and 168% DDoS increase data, but ATLAS scale exceeds Radware’s telemetry footprint.

3

Radware’s unified WAAP + DDoS positioning is stronger than NETSCOUT’s in platform consolidation deals

NETSCOUT has no WAF, bot management, or API security. In enterprise security consolidation cycles, Radware offers DDoS + WAAP + API security under one platform. NETSCOUT can only compete on the DDoS component. This structural advantage grows as enterprises reduce point-solution vendors.

4

In DDoS-specific evaluations, NETSCOUT is Radware’s most technically capable competitor

When DDoS is evaluated independently (carrier/ISP, critical infrastructure, government), NETSCOUT’s ISP-grade heritage, ATLAS telemetry, and multi-layer stack (AED + Cloud + Sightline + TMS) represent the deepest pure-play alternative. Radware’s differentiation in these evaluations relies on encrypted DDoS mitigation (per Radware claim) and hybrid deployment flexibility — though Corero’s October 2025 SmartWall ONE Encrypted launch (no-decryption per Corero claim) means encrypted-L7 is no longer unique to Radware. Cloud scale (Radware 30 Tbps vs NETSCOUT 15+ Tbps), per-appliance throughput (DefensePro 800 Gbps), and ERT remain stronger Radware differentiators.

5

NETSCOUT’s ISP channel is not addressable by Radware and should not be a competitive focus

NETSCOUT’s Arbor Sightline + TMS is embedded in Tier-1 ISP backbones (Arelion, 16+ year relationships). This is a different market from Radware’s enterprise and service provider focus. Radware should not invest in competing for ISP backbone DDoS monitoring — the switching costs and integration depth make this a non-addressable segment.

6

Rising DDoS attack complexity benefits both NETSCOUT and Radware at the expense of platform vendors

NETSCOUT’s threat report documents 30 Tbps attacks, 42% multi-vector, 219% AI tool growth, and campaign coordination among 65+ threat actors. As DDoS attacks become more sophisticated and AI-augmented, enterprise buyers may reconsider whether bundled DDoS from Cloudflare/Akamai is sufficient vs. dedicated DDoS from NETSCOUT or Radware. Both NETSCOUT and Radware benefit from this complexity trend.
