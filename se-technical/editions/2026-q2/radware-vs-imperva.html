Market & Positioning

Radware

Radware is a security-first application and network protection vendor selling primarily to enterprises and carriers/service providers rather than SMB. Its delivery model is hybrid: on-premises appliances (DefensePro for DDoS, AppWall for WAF) paired with a cloud scrubbing-center network and the fully managed Cloud Application Protection Service (Cloud WAF, API Protection, Bot Manager, client-side protection). Core strength is behavioral, ML-driven detection that auto-generates and adapts policies with low false positives, plus deep DDoS expertise and an Emergency Response Team. Hybrid deployment with real-time on-prem mitigation handing off to cloud is a genuine differentiator for latency-sensitive and carrier customers. Known weaknesses: a much smaller global edge/CDN footprint than Cloudflare or Akamai, no built-in CDN, lower brand mindshare in cloud-native and developer segments, contact-sales-only pricing with no self-serve or free tier, and a portal/UX that trails the hyperscale-style consoles of larger rivals. Best fit: security-led enterprises and telcos that value detection accuracy and hybrid control over edge scale.

Imperva

Imperva, a Thales company, is an application and data security specialist targeting mid-market and enterprise customers, with notable strength in regulated industries and the public sector. Its WAF is deployment-flexible — Cloud WAF (formerly Incapsula), on-premises and virtual gateways, and the newer Kubernetes-based Elastic WAF — giving a true hybrid and on-prem option that purely cloud-native rivals lack. Imperva pairs application protection (WAF, DDoS, API Security, Advanced Bot Protection) with a leading data-security portfolio, and Thales adds encryption and key management for an applications-to-data story. Strengths: long-standing WAF pedigree (repeated Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader and a Leader in the Forrester Wave WAF Q1 2025), deployment flexibility, and data-security integration. Weaknesses: a smaller global scrubbing/edge network than Cloudflare or Akamai; contact-sales, usage-based pricing with no free tier and limited cost transparency; a fragmented product/console history from multiple acquisitions; and uncertainty around a GA LLM/AI firewall offering. Best fit: enterprises wanting unified application and data security across hybrid estates.

Battlecard — Radware vs. Imperva

Where Radware Wins

  • Dedicated DDoS appliance line: DefensePro provides always-on on-prem behavioral mitigation feeding cloud scrubbing. Imperva is cloud-scrubbing only with no on-prem DDoS appliance, so its hybrid story is limited compared with appliance-based vendors - even though its WAF is hybrid.
  • Behavioral, signature-free DDoS and faster mitigation: DefensePro detects volumetric, protocol and encrypted L7 floods with patented behavioral algorithms; Radware advertises near-instant behavioral mitigation versus Imperva's 3-second L3/L4 SLA.
  • Carrier-grade and non-HTTP depth: DefensePro and Cloud DDoS protect DNS, UDP and other non-HTTP protocols with behavioral detection and integrate into carrier in-network deployments - a service-provider fit Imperva's proxy-centric model does not target.
  • First-party threat intelligence built for DDoS: Radware's Threat Intelligence Subscriptions and the ERT Active Attackers Feed are curated by Radware's Threat Research Center and feed both Cloud WAF and DefensePro with real-time, DDoS-focused intelligence.
  • Unified, security-first portfolio without acquisition drag: Radware's WAAP, Bot Manager, API Security and LLM Firewall share one policy model and portal. Imperva carries a fragmented product/console history from multiple acquisitions.
  • Managed Emergency Response Team: Radware backs Cloud WAF and DDoS with a human ERT for managed and emergency response and post-attack forensics - a service depth beyond Imperva's managed rulesets.
  • Patented bot defense UX: Intent-based Deep Behavior Analysis plus a blockchain-inspired Crypto Challenge gives a CAPTCHA-free, resource-exhausting mitigation; Imperva still relies on optional CAPTCHA and has no dedicated branded invisible-challenge product.

Where Imperva Wins

  • Strongest WAF analyst pedigree: a repeated Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader and a Leader in the Forrester Wave WAF Q1 2025 - a credibility asset in analyst-driven enterprise evaluations.
  • Applications-to-data security story: Imperva pairs WAF, DDoS, API and bot with a leading data-security portfolio, and parent Thales adds encryption and key management - a unified app-and-data narrative Radware cannot match.
  • WAF deployment flexibility for DevOps: Cloud WAF, on-prem/virtual gateways and the Kubernetes-native Elastic WAF deploy in any cloud or on-prem, with default policies that 97% of customers run unmodified (95% in blocking mode).
  • Mature, well-documented API security: continuous discovery with data sensitivity classification, posture assessment with developer-ready fixes, API Security Testing in CI/CD, and REST/GraphQL/gRPC coverage.
  • Larger global scrubbing/PoP footprint: ~13 Tbps across ~49 PoPs processing tens of billions of packets per second - a broader cloud network than Radware's ~21 centers.

Competitive Landmines

  • Imperva is the Gartner and Forrester WAF Leader - Radware isn't even in the conversation. — Analyst placement reflects market presence, not detection accuracy for a specific deal. Radware pairs the same positive-security plus signature model as Imperva but adds auto-generated granular per-zone policy and DAST-driven virtual patching - and crucially a dedicated DDoS appliance line Imperva does not have. Evaluate on a PoC against the customer's real traffic, not on a quadrant.
  • Imperva gives you application AND data security in one platform - Radware only does the app layer. — The data-security story is real, but most WAAP/DDoS evaluations are scoped to application and network protection, where Radware's behavioral DDoS, hybrid appliances and ERT lead. If data security is genuinely in scope it is a separate buying motion - it should not decide the application-protection layer.
  • Imperva's WAF is hybrid too, with Elastic WAF for Kubernetes - Radware has no edge advantage. — Imperva's WAF is hybrid, but its DDoS protection is cloud-scrubbing only with no on-prem appliance. Radware delivers always-on on-prem DDoS mitigation via DefensePro with behavioral, signature-free detection of encrypted and non-HTTP floods - the hybrid DDoS layer Imperva cannot match.
  • Imperva's LLM/AI security is already shipping - Radware is behind on AI. — Imperva's AI Application Security datasheet still carries a BETA label as of December 2025. Radware's LLM Firewall launched GA in November 2025, and the Agentic AI Protection solution (Feb 2026) extends to agentic threats including memory-poisoning - Radware is at least level on this emerging pillar, not behind.

Ideal Deal Profile

An enterprise or service provider where DDoS is a first-class requirement - financial services, telco, gaming or e-commerce facing volumetric, encrypted L7 or non-HTTP (DNS/UDP) attacks - that needs always-on on-prem appliance mitigation feeding cloud scrubbing and a managed ERT. They value detection accuracy and a unified, security-first portfolio over an analyst quadrant or a data-security bundle, and are wary of Imperva's cloud-only DDoS model and its acquisition-fragmented product and console history.

Imperva wins on WAF analyst pedigree and the Thales data-security story, but Radware wins any deal where on-prem DDoS appliance hybrid, behavioral detection accuracy and a managed Emergency Response Team are what the customer actually needs.
