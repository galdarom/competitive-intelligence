Delta Analysis (vs. March 2026)

What Accelerated

AI Inference Cloud — Akamai’s Boldest Bet

The AI Grid launch at GTC represents Akamai’s most ambitious strategic move since the Linode acquisition. Jensen Huang’s keynote endorsement, multi-thousand GPU clusters at core, and intelligent orchestration across 4,400+ edge locations signal a genuine attempt to reposition from CDN/security into distributed AI compute. The $200M four-year deal validates enterprise demand.

Security Segment Growth Momentum

Security revenue at $592M (+11% Q4) continues to be the reliable growth engine. API Security crossed the $100M run-rate threshold with >100% YoY growth. Guardicore Segmentation acceleration (+36%) confirms Zero Trust demand. Security now represents 54% of Q4 revenue.

NVIDIA Partnership Deepening

Two major NVIDIA collaborations in 60 days: AI Grid (Blackwell GPUs for inference) and BlueField DPUs (OT Zero Trust). No other security/edge vendor has this depth of NVIDIA partnership. Positions Akamai uniquely at the intersection of AI compute and security.

Agentic Commerce Security

Visa partnership to secure AI shopping agents across 175M merchant locations is a first-mover play in a new category. Combines Akamai’s bot detection expertise with Visa’s Trusted Agent Protocol for the emerging agentic economy.

What Is New

Guardicore AI-Powered Policy Automation

AI-driven application understanding and proof-driven enforcement are net-new capabilities. Automated policy generation + simulation before enforcement addresses the #1 barrier to Zero Trust adoption: policy complexity.

Fermyon / WebAssembly at Edge

Fermyon acquisition (Dec 2025) adds serverless WebAssembly FaaS. A direct competitive response to Cloudflare Workers, aiming to attract developers to Akamai’s edge for application workloads beyond CDN.

$200M AI Cluster Contract

A single four-year, $200M deal for a metro-edge GPU cluster validates that enterprises are willing to pay for distributed AI inference outside hyperscaler clouds. Early adoption in gaming, finance, and media.

What Slowed or Faces Risk

Delivery Revenue in Structural Decline

Delivery at $311M (-2% YoY) continues to erode as traditional CDN becomes commoditized. Management expects “further deterioration” in FY2026. Not offset by growth in other segments yet in absolute terms.

Profitability Under Pressure

2026 profitability guidance fell below consensus due to AI infrastructure investment (Blackwell GPU procurement, co-location costs). CapEx rising significantly. The inference bet requires sustained investment before returns materialize.

Cloudflare’s Platform Expansion Threat

Cloudflare’s Agents Week demonstrated that WAAP/DDoS is now “bundled free” within a platform sale. At 500 Tbps and 330+ cities, Cloudflare’s network now exceeds Akamai’s in raw Anycast capacity. Akamai must differentiate on more than network scale.

Meaningful Pattern Shifts

Akamai’s Three-Pivot Identity

CDN company → security company → AI infrastructure company. The AI Grid + Fermyon + CIS growth (+45%) collectively signal a third strategic reinvention. Success depends on whether Akamai can maintain security growth while simultaneously building a credible AI compute business against hyperscaler competition.

Edge Becomes AI Infrastructure

Akamai’s 4,400+ edge locations are being repurposed from content delivery to AI inference. This transforms the CDN asset from a depreciating legacy into a potential AI moat. If the thesis holds, Akamai’s geographic distribution becomes a structural advantage no hyperscaler can easily replicate.

Strategic Signals

Signal 1: The Edge-to-AI Pivot Is Akamai’s Existential Bet

Akamai is spending $250M+ on NVIDIA Blackwell GPU infrastructure, signed a $200M four-year CIS contract, and expects 45–50% cloud revenue growth in 2026. This is not a side project — it is the central strategic thesis. If Akamai successfully repositions its 4,400+ edge locations as distributed AI infrastructure, it creates a moat that hyperscalers cannot easily replicate (they are concentrated in fewer, larger data centers). If it fails, the investment drags profitability without offsetting delivery decline. For Radware, this means Akamai’s attention and R&D allocation are increasingly directed toward compute, potentially creating openings in security innovation.

Signal 2: Akamai’s API Security Dominance Is Accelerating

API Security exceeded $100M run rate with >100% YoY growth. The SOTI report (87% incident rate, 258 attacks/day per enterprise) creates demand that feeds directly into App & API Protector sales. Akamai’s API security thought leadership (SOTI reports, impact surveys) is the most effective demand-generation engine in the market. Radware’s Pynt acquisition (pre-production API testing) offers differentiation Akamai lacks, but Radware must counter Akamai’s research-driven narrative with comparable market evidence.

Signal 3: The Agentic Commerce Opportunity Is Real — and Akamai Got There First

The Visa partnership for AI shopping agents positions Akamai at the intersection of bot management and commerce infrastructure. AI bot traffic surged 300% per Akamai’s own data. Akamai’s decade of bot management expertise is directly transferable to the agentic commerce challenge (distinguishing legitimate AI agents from malicious bots). No other security vendor has this level of partnership with a payments network. For Radware, the competitive implication is that Akamai is extending bot management into commerce — a market Radware does not currently address.

Signal 4: Akamai’s OT Security Play Opens a New TAM

Guardicore + NVIDIA BlueField for “un-agentable” industrial equipment addresses critical infrastructure sectors (energy, transportation, manufacturing) now under regulatory pressure. This is a category where neither Cloudflare, Fastly, nor Radware competes. Akamai’s enterprise sales motion and existing Guardicore relationships in large enterprises create a natural go-to-market for OT security. The Q2 2026 GA date means this becomes a revenue contributor in H2 2026.

Signal 5: The Revenue vs. Valuation Gap Tells the Strategic Story

Akamai generates 2x Cloudflare’s revenue ($4.21B vs. $2.17B) but carries 1/5th the market cap ($13.9B vs. ~$74B). The market is pricing Akamai as an infrastructure company in transition and Cloudflare as a platform company with momentum. Akamai must demonstrate that the AI inference pivot + security growth can rerate the stock. For Radware, this valuation gap means Cloudflare can afford to give security away as a loss leader, while Akamai cannot — which paradoxically makes Akamai a more predictable competitor on security pricing.

Signal 6: Delivery Decline Creates an Urgency Clock

CDN delivery revenue ($311M Q4, -2% YoY) is in structural decline as the market commoditizes. Management expects further deterioration. Akamai has a finite window to grow CIS and security fast enough to offset delivery erosion. 2026 guidance (5–8% total growth) implies CIS and security must collectively grow ~$350–550M to overcome delivery losses and reach the high end. If CIS growth stalls or AI investment costs exceed returns, Akamai risks flat or declining total revenue. This urgency may make Akamai more aggressive on pricing and M&A in security markets where Radware competes.

Executive Takeaways

1

Akamai is undergoing its third strategic reinvention

CDN → security → AI infrastructure. The Inference Cloud / AI Grid / Fermyon investments signal that Akamai views its future as a distributed compute provider, not just a security or CDN company. Security remains the cash engine (53% of revenue, +10% growth), but R&D and CapEx are increasingly allocated to AI compute. For Radware, this dual focus means Akamai may be slower to innovate on pure WAAP/DDoS while pursuing the AI pivot.

2

Akamai’s API security momentum is the most direct competitive threat to Radware

With >$100M API Security run rate, >100% growth, and research-driven demand generation (SOTI reports, impact surveys), Akamai is establishing itself as the enterprise API security standard. Radware’s Pynt acquisition (pre-production testing) offers unique differentiation, but integration maturity must accelerate to compete with Akamai’s runtime-first approach.

3

The NVIDIA partnership creates a competitive moat in enterprise infrastructure

No other security/edge vendor has dual NVIDIA partnerships (AI Grid for compute, BlueField for OT security). This opens TAM in industrial/OT security and AI inference that Radware does not currently address. The Akamai + NVIDIA relationship is a durable competitive asset that deepens over time.

4

Radware’s encrypted DDoS and hybrid deployment model remain defensible against Akamai

Akamai Prolexic is best-in-class for enterprise DDoS but requires traffic decryption. Radware’s no-decrypt L7 DDoS capability and on-prem/hybrid deployment flexibility address use cases where Akamai’s cloud-first model faces friction (certificate-sensitive environments, regulated industries, data sovereignty requirements).

5

Akamai’s delivery decline creates pricing pressure across security

As CDN revenue erodes, Akamai has increasing incentive to bundle security aggressively with infrastructure deals to retain customers. Expect more “security included with CDN/compute” packaging. Radware should anticipate downward pricing pressure from Akamai in deals where both vendors compete, particularly in large enterprise accounts where Akamai has existing CDN relationships.

6

Akamai’s bot management expertise extends into agentic commerce — a market Radware doesn’t address

The Visa partnership positions Akamai uniquely at the intersection of security and payments for AI agents. As agentic commerce matures, the distinction between “bot management” and “agent authentication” will blur. Radware’s Agentic AI Protection focuses on runtime security of agents themselves — a complementary but different category than securing commerce from agents.
