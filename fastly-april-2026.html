Annual Revenue Retention Slight Dip

Annual Revenue Retention Rate fell from 99.0% (FY2024) to 98.7% (FY2025). Fastly is deprecating this metric in favor of LTM NRR (which rose to 110%). The dip is small, but the metric switch should be watched — vendors typically retire metrics when they're no longer favorable.

Platform Breadth Remains Narrow

Fastly's product portfolio is still: CDN + Next-Gen WAF + DDoS + Compute&Edge. No equivalent to Cloudflare's developer platform (Workers/R2/D1/KV/Durable Objects), Akamai's Inference Cloud, or Imperva's data security stack. Differentiation must come from Forrester-recognized developer experience and AI bot research.

FY2025 results inverted that: first non-GAAP profitable year, +15% revenue growth, Q4 +23%, RPO +55%, LTM NRR 110%, customer count +32. The change in market perception is more important than the absolute numbers. Stock +354% reflects a narrative re-rating. Radware and other competitors should no longer dismiss Fastly as “perpetually weak” — it is now a credible enterprise platform with operating leverage.

Signal 2: AI Bot Research Establishes Fastly as a Thought Leader

The December 2025 Threat Insights Report (49% bot traffic, 99% unwanted; Meta 60% of AI crawler traffic; ChatGPT 68% of fetcher traffic) and August research on AI crawlers position Fastly uniquely on the agentic-web question. The thesis — “simple block/allow tactics are obsolete in the agentic age” — is differentiated and product-aligned. Compared to Cloudflare's AI Crawl Control and Akamai's Visa partnership, Fastly's research-led approach is a different (potentially more credible) GTM motion. For Radware, this means competing in bot management increasingly requires research-driven thought leadership, not just product features.

Signal 3: Forrester Edge Development Platforms Wave Leader Is Meaningful Validation

Forrester named Fastly a Leader in Edge Development Platforms Q1 2026 — the first major analyst recognition that values Fastly's developer experience and Wasm performance. The 35.4µs WebAssembly cold start outperforms container- and JavaScript-based serverless alternatives. The competitive risk for Radware: Fastly's small but enterprise-anchored customer base is highly defensible — difficult to displace via price competition alone.

Executive Takeaways

1

Fastly is now profitable — the long-standing competitive narrative needs an update

FY2025 first non-GAAP profitable year. Q4 +23% revenue. LTM NRR 110%. Customer count +32. Stock +354%. The “Fastly is structurally weak” framing is no longer accurate. Competitive sales conversations should reflect Fastly as a financially disciplined, recognized edge platform — not a struggling challenger.

2

Fastly's narrow platform remains a structural risk vs. Cloudflare/Akamai

No developer platform breadth (no Workers/R2/D1/KV equivalent), no AI inference offering (vs. Akamai AI Grid), no AI security stack (vs. Cloudflare AI Security for Apps, Imperva AI Application Security, Radware LLM Firewall). The narrow product scope simplifies the sales motion but caps TAM exposure. Radware's WAAP + DDoS + API + AI security platform is meaningfully broader than Fastly's CDN+WAF+Compute scope.

3

AI bot research is Fastly's most differentiated GTM asset — competitors should respond with research, not features

Two Threat Insights Reports in 9 months (Aug 2025, Dec 2025) plus “intent-based bot strategy” positioning give Fastly a research-led narrative. Cloudflare and Akamai counter with product (AI Crawl Control, Visa partnership). Radware's response should be its own AI bot research program leveraging Bot Manager and Agentic AI Protection telemetry — product features alone won't counter the credibility built by sustained primary research.

4

Forrester Edge Leader is meaningful but vertical-specific

Forrester Wave Edge Development Platforms Q1 2026 Leader recognition validates Compute@Edge platform for developer-led deals. This is most relevant in technology, financial services, and media customers who care about edge compute performance (35.4µs WebAssembly cold start). For most enterprise security buyers, Forrester WAAP / Gartner WAAP MQ matters more — where Imperva (Leader) and Akamai (Leader) hold the strongest positions.

5

LALIGA partnership signals vertical strategy — watch for similar deals in media/sports/entertainment

The LALIGA AI anti-piracy deal is Fastly's first major sports rights-holder partnership of its kind. Pattern to watch: similar partnerships with Premier League, NBA, NHL, or major broadcasters. This vertical strategy targets segments where edge compute + low latency are acute differentiators. Radware does not currently address sports/media piracy use cases and would face high entry barriers if this becomes a contested vertical.

6

Q1 2026 earnings May 6 is the next inflection point

Fastly Q1 2026 earnings drop May 6 (3 days from this brief publication). Guide: $168–174M revenue (+18% YoY at midpoint), gross margin ~64%, non-GAAP EPS $0.07–0.10. If Fastly delivers ≥18% growth and confirms profitability, the platform re-rating thesis strengthens. If Q1 misses, the “narrative recovery” theme weakens and prior “narrowly differentiated” framing returns. Watch for: NRR sustained ≥108%, RPO continuing to grow faster than revenue, and any new AI/security product disclosures.
